HEALTH ACROSS THE world

Life Expectancy

Life expectancy serves as a vital measure of a population's overall health and well-being. Influenced by factors such as healthcare access, lifestyle choices, and environmental conditions, disparities exist worldwide. Some nations achieve life expectancies surpassing 80 years, whereas others lag behind at under 60 years. It's imperative to grasp these differences and collaboratively strive to elevate health standards globally.

BMI

Exploring the trends of the top 10 countries with the highest BMI over the years provides a glimpse into the overall fitness and weight management of countries. As dietary habits, lifestyle choices, and health policies shift, this bump chart provides an insight into how nations grapple with weight-related health challenges. Understanding these patterns can guide global health initiatives and awareness campaigns.

Evidently, some countries seem to top the charts year after year, which may suggest either lifestyle issues or a lack of awareness. In contrast, other countries seem to be improving their BMI over the years, which may suggest that they are taking steps to improve their health.

Life Expectancy vs GDP

The relationship between a nation's GDP and the life expectancy of its citizens is complex. This bubble chart compares GDP with life expectancy, highlighting that while economic prosperity often correlates with better health outcomes, it's not the sole determinant. Multiple factors influence the health and longevity of populations, emphasising the diverse challenges in global health.

The bubble chart also highlights the differences between regions, where it seems that some continents such as Africa generally sit on the lower end of both life expectancy as well as GDP per capita. This suggests that as a whole, improved wealth can definitely help increase the potential for better life expectancies.

However, it is also important to note that on the higher end, countries with similar GDP per capita still have differing life expectancies. This may be due to other external factors.
